Sustainable Brands

Indian brands doing fashion the right way — for people and the planet.

Next time you shop, consider these homegrown brands that prioritise ethics, artisans, and the environment.

01

Maati

Udaipur, Rajasthan

A PETA-verified sustainable brand by Neha Kabre, known for skin-friendly, eco-friendly garments. Follows a zero-waste policy using natural raw materials and upcycled yarn, coordinating with local craftsmen across cultures.

Visit Maati
02

Doodlage

Founded 2012

Founded by Kriti Tula, Doodlage upcycles leftover fabrics from large manufacturers. Uses organic materials like banana fibres, cotton and corn. Minimises production waste by turning leftovers into bags and home furnishings.

Visit Doodlage
03

Okhai

Rural India (Tata Chemicals)

A rural women's collective set up by Tata Chemicals Society For Rural Development (est. 2002). Handcrafted apparel and lifestyle products by rural artisans, using skills unique to their culture. Provides contemporary eco-friendly designs.

Visit Okhai
04

No Nasties

Goa

Founded by Apurva Kothari, No Nasties creates fashionable, durable apparel from 100% organic cotton sourced from fair-trade farmers. No harmful chemicals, no pesticides, no genetically modified seeds — just clean, honest fashion.

Visit No Nasties
05

The Indian Ethnic Co.

Mumbai, Maharashtra

Founded in 2016 by Hetal Desai and daughters Twaara and Lekhinee. Started as an Instagram business, now a celebrated brand that honours Indian weavers and artisans across the nation with every piece they create.

Visit The Indian Ethnic Co.
